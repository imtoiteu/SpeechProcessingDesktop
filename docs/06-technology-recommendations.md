> These are **evidence-weighted starting points to benchmark**, not finalized choices. Every recommendation
> names its trade-off and what would change it. Per `doubt-driven-development`, these were adversarially reviewed.

## Rationale (key non-obvious points)

- **Do not default to PhoWhisper-large.** The paper's own table (Table 2): CMV-Vi WER large **8.14** vs medium **8.27** (gap 0.13), VIVOS **4.67** vs **4.97** (0.30), VLSP-T1 **13.75** vs **14.12** (0.37) — marginal gains for ~2× the compute. The "largest is best" assumption is not supported. These exact figures were **re-verified against the primary source (two independent renderings) during the doubt-review**, after an adversarial reviewer alleged a ~1.4 WER gap; that allegation did not survive the primary source. [VERIFIED — paper](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2406.02555), [GitHub README](https://github.com/VinAIResearch/PhoWhisper)
- **ChunkFormer-Large-Vie is now a co-primary ASR candidate (project confirmed non-commercial, 2026-06-23).** 110M params (≈14× smaller than PhoWhisper-large), natively streaming, CTC timestamps, with author-reported CMV-Vi 6.66 / VIVOS 4.18 that *exceed* PhoWhisper-large on those sets. The former blocker (CC-BY-NC) no longer applies. Two things still gate it: (1) the cross-paper comparison uses different splits/normalization → validate under **identical eval** before trusting the win; (2) **cross-platform runtime support** (Win/Linux/servers + Apple Silicon) is less mature than Whisper's — must be confirmed, since portability is still required. Its native streaming + CTC timestamps could *simplify* the architecture (no LocalAgreement emulation, more reliable SRT/VTT timing). [VERIFIED card](https://huggingface.co/khanhld/chunkformer-large-vie)
- **Engine choice is a portability decision, not just a speed one.** faster-whisper is CPU-only on macOS; MLX is Apple-only; whisper.cpp is the only GPU-everywhere option. The abstraction is mandatory so the choice stays reversible and benchmarkable. **Caveat:** the abstraction is only as portable as the model artifacts — no pre-built PhoWhisper GGML/MLX exists, so the Mac-accel fallback hinges on an unproven conversion (gating spike in Phase 1). [VERIFIED](https://opennmt.net/CTranslate2/hardware_support.html)
- **License is no longer the binding constraint — portability is (decision: non-commercial, 2026-06-23).** CC-BY-NC / gated / non-compete components (ChunkFormer, nguyenvulebinh wav2vec2, VIVOS, VLSP, TEN VAD, SimulStreaming/AlignAtt) are now usable in the product. Licenses are still *recorded* (in case intent ever changes), but they no longer disqualify candidates. The remaining hard filter is **cross-platform portability** (Apple Silicon + Windows/Linux/servers) — every candidate must clear it.
- **Correction layer is the most questionable proposed component.** It adds a second model, memory, and latency, while the (mixed, mostly non-Vietnamese) evidence base predicts low-or-negative ROI on a strong PhoWhisper baseline — *defer pending the A/B benchmark* rather than treat the harm as settled. Cheapest high-value experiment: prove or kill it early ([05](05-benchmark-methodology.md) comparison #6).

## What is explicitly NOT recommended yet
- Committing to any single engine before the Phase-1 benchmark.
- Shipping the correction LLM in v1.
- Adopting an Apple-only runtime as the default (portability still required even though license isn't a blocker).
- Declaring ChunkFormer the winner on cross-paper numbers — its accuracy claim and its cross-platform runtime support must both be validated first.
